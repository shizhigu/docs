## Overview

SkoutLab reports are designed for decision-makers. Every report answers: **"What should I know, and what should I do about it?"**

## Report Components

### Executive Summary

The top of every report includes:

- **Key Findings** — The 3-5 most important discoveries
- **Overall Assessment** — High-level status (opportunity, risk, stable)
- **Quick Actions** — Immediate steps to consider

### Findings List

Each finding includes:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Status" icon="circle-check">
    Confirmed, Refuted, or Inconclusive
  </Card>
  <Card title="Impact" icon="gauge-high">
    Business significance score
  </Card>
  <Card title="Evidence" icon="chart-line">
    Supporting charts and statistics
  </Card>
  <Card title="Actions" icon="list-check">
    Recommended next steps
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Evidence & Charts

For each finding, click to expand:

- **Visualizations** — Auto-generated charts showing the pattern
- **Statistics** — P-values, confidence intervals, effect sizes
- **Data Samples** — Representative examples from your data

## Working with Reports

### Exploring Findings

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review Summary">
    Start with the executive summary for the big picture.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter by Impact">
    Focus on Critical and High impact findings first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Expand Details">
    Click any finding to see full evidence and methodology.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask Follow-ups">
    Click "Investigate Further" to create a new focused analysis.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Sharing Reports

Reports can be shared with stakeholders:

| Method | Use Case |
|--------|----------|
| **Link** | Share directly with team members |
| **PDF Export** | For presentations and emails |
| **Scheduled** | Automated weekly/monthly reports (coming soon) |

## Report History

All reports are saved and searchable:

- Compare findings over time
- Track hypothesis validation history
- Reference past analyses in new tasks

<Tip>
When asking a question, SkoutLab considers previous findings. This builds institutional memory — insights compound over time.
</Tip>
